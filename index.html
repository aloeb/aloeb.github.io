Projects

Interested in the kind of stuff I do? Well you can check it all out here! It's pretty much a basic list of my bigger more successful projects. You can check out other projects of mine on GitHub. I've listed them in order of importance (in my mind), so they aren't nessessarily chronological.

OneUp

For my Sofware Engineering course, my team of six made a social media app, OneUp! It was on iOS and Android, and the premise is that you can set records around you, then "OneUp" other's records to introduce a little competition into everyday life. Development has paused for the summer, but we may be picking it back up come fall to clean things up. The coolest feature I got to work on was live video filters (like stopwatches and the like). I'm definitely going to see if I can polish up the code and make it work even better.

Flappy Keyboard

Flappy bird was a super fun iOS keyboard I made. I was really hoping to release it to the iOS store, but it was rejected for not actually inputing text. Go figure.

Tempo

Tempo has been my favorite hackathon project so far! It's been shown that people can exercise more effectively when syncing their movements to music. We set out to simplify the process by syncing music to people's movements. Utilizing the Pebble and the Spotify API, we were able to intelligently select music as you moved around. Whether you want to use it while running, or just going for a stroll, the app could match your tempo every time!

Just 25

Just 25 was my very first, and most successful (so far), Android app. I made it with a friend, and it was a blast! We were just learning Android at the time, so we are just now trying to go back and polish it up a bit! Feel free to check it out by clicking on the picture!

Fruitful Feats

Fruitful Feats was my second Android app! This one was made by a team of seven which I project managed. But I didn't just project manage! I also did a bunch of coding on this project. The organization was one big challenge, but the biggest challenge was the time contraints! Most of us were leaving for college, so we only had five weeks to make the app work and look good! Although it's not perfect, I'm happy with the final product given our timeframe. Check it out by clicking the image!

TypeMuse

TypeMuse is the typing tutorial the world needs! No more boring tutorials where you type words for hours on end! TypeMuse lets you type along to the lyrics of your favorite songs while the tunes play in the background. This project actually won the TechOlympics Showcase, placed first at the UC 2014 IT Expo, and went on to win a 2015 statewide business pitch competition! We wanted to continue development and potentially release TypeMuse to the public, but unfortunately, the high costs and legal implications that went along with using copyrighted music ended up being more than what we wanted to deal with.

MealMatchr

MealMatchr was a cool iOS app that my team created at my first Hackathon, BoilerMake. The app would quickly connect you with students with similar interests in the closest dining court so that you could meet new people and avoid dining alone when all of your friends were busy in class! The app was written entirely in Swift with a PHP/MySQL backend. I took care of all the application code as I had the most experience with mobile development.

Grade Texts

Halfway through my senior year in high school, I saw a problem. Logging into the school's grades website was a hassel and took way longer than it should have. In addition, the interface was not designed for mobile, and what was a student to do if they did not have a smart phone? My solution was Grade Texts! Written in C#, this server side app would go through a list of students and send them their grades on a regular basis. I even got to meet and propose my app to the school district's IT department!
